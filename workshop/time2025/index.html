Overview

The TIME 2025 workshop represents a pioneering initiative dedicated to advancing cross-domain knowledge exchange and methodological adaptation in contemporary web technologies. In the current digital ecosystem, characterized by multifaceted challenges spanning from privacy vulnerabilities to algorithmic biases, there exists a paramount necessity for integrating successful methodologies across disparate domains. This workshop bridges the gap between academic research and industry applications, providing a unique platform where experts and professionals can share insights on social network analysis, graph algorithms, web mining, security, privacy, and ethics. Through facilitating comprehensive evaluations and empirical guidance across these domains, TIME 2025 aims to bridge the theoretical-practical divide, translating cutting-edge research into actionable solutions for modern web challenges, setting a new standard for interdisciplinary collaboration in web technologies.

Invited Speakers

Assoc. Prof. Wei Liu
H-index: 28, Citations: 3,957
University of Western Australia, Australia

Dr Liu received her PhD from the University of Newcastle, Australia in 2003. She is now a full time teaching & research academic in the Department of Computer Science and Software Engineering at the University of Western Australia. She leads the UWA Centre for Natural and Technical Language Processing, with research effort focusing on knowledge discovery from natural language text (NLP), generative AI, LLM-based semantic technologies, deep learning methods for knowledge graph construction and analysis, as well as sequential data mining and forecasting. Her recent work includes training machine learning models from fusing multi-modality heterogeneous data, and neural-symbolic computation with human-in-the-loop. Her industry-related research projects include knowledge graph refinement for geological survey reports, technical language processing on maintenance work orders, incident/safety log analysis and visualization, short-term traffic prediction, and clinical data integration and analysis in ophthalmology.

Assoc. Prof. Mohammad Abdur Razzaque
H-index: 31, Citations: 4,793
